A 5 foot tall, 1.5 kilogram rocket my classmate Emmett Hart and I built for our 2020 Intermediate Engineering class final project. The rocket was an EZ-I65 airframe equipped with a payload bay, a Raspberry Pi (a single board computer running Linux), a custom PCB I designed to hold our sensors and connect them to the Raspberry Pi's GPIO pins, and an IMU, temperature, and altitude sensor. The rocket flew on an H283 high power composite fuel rocket motor making 73 pounds of thrust, and the launch earned me my NAR Junior Level 1 high power rocketry certification.

A device I designed and built that replaces the buffer of AR-pattern rifles with a polymer housing containing a microcontroller, high-G accelerometer, and bluetooth radio. The device is currently a working prototype and is intended to enable data-driven adjustment of rifle gassing for recoil mitigation and fire rate tuning. I have completed a new microcontroller design and am currently debugging an ADXL373 breakout board I designed for the project.

I am an active contributer to the open-source digital night vision project from Vonscherf Arms called the PVS-69A. The device uses starlight cameras and off-the-shelf components assembled in a 3D printed housing which can be assembled in either a monocular or binocular configuration, and beats current digital night vision devices on the market in low light sensitivity, FOV, latency, and configurability. My contributions so far have been to PCB design.
